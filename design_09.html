01

Business Challenges

A decade of organic growth had outpaced the organization's internal governance capacity. As the B2B segment scaled alongside existing B2C branches,
four structural risks converged — each compounding the others in ways leadership could no longer resolve reactively.

Scalability Risk

No System. No Visibility. No Control.

Data lived in Zalo groups, Excel files, and the memory of individual managers. There was no centralized mechanism to collect, synthesize, or surface operational data — making it impossible to detect problems before they escalated across branches and business units.

Risk: operational blind spots would become irrecoverable at B2B scale, threatening the viability of the new growth segment.

Academic Governance Risk

Teaching Commitments with No Verification Layer

No system tracked whether classroom delivery aligned with syllabi, teaching plans, or contractual obligations. For B2B school partnerships — where academic standards are a formal commitment — this absence created measurable reputational and commercial risk.

Risk: quality degradation would go undetected until it damaged client relationships and contract renewals.

Resource Allocation Risk

Workforce Visibility at Near-Zero

Teaching hours, coverage patterns, and staff utilization were not tracked. The organization had no basis for informed staffing decisions — creating both emergency recruitment gaps and invisible overstaffing that inflated costs without detection.

Risk: inefficient resource deployment with no corrective signal until a crisis occurred.

Financial Control Risk

Payroll Built on Manual Estimation

Without verified hour logs, payroll was calculated manually — prone to error, slow to process, and nearly impossible to audit when disputes arose. Branch-level cost analysis was not feasible, limiting leadership's ability to forecast or control the organization's largest expense.

Risk: unresolvable disputes, eroding staff trust, and uncontrolled payroll liability at scale.

02

My Role & Ownership

One person. End-to-end. From problem diagnosis to production deployment.
No dedicated team, no technical mentor, no internal precedent — full system lifecycle ownership, beginning from zero familiarity with Google Workspace development and without any formal background in technology, systems design, or finance.

01 / Diagnosis

Problem Mapping

Conducted structured discovery across all operational functions to identify interconnected failure points — not just surface symptoms — and translated operational pain into solvable system requirements.

02 / Design

Solution Architecture

Evaluated feasible approaches under a zero-budget constraint. Designed a three-layer system architecture that reused existing Google Workspace access while introducing structured data flows and automation.

03 / Build

Tool Development

Built all templates, automated scripts (Google Apps Script), reporting pipelines, and payroll automation tooling. No external developer involved.

04 / Deploy

Phased Rollout

Piloted across 3 B2C branches to validate and refine, then expanded to all 11 locations — managing change resistance from teaching staff throughout the transition.

05 / Enable

Training & Documentation

Authored full user documentation and produced hands-on video tutorials to ensure adoption and reduce dependency on myself for ongoing operations.

06 / Sustain

Maintenance & Iteration

Monitored system performance post-launch and incorporated user feedback. Maintained all tooling and resolved operational issues as the system matured.

03

Solution Architecture

Built entirely within Google Workspace — treating Google Sheets not as a spreadsheet tool but as a structured data collection and aggregation layer.
The architecture establishes three distinct governance layers, each with its own scope, responsible party, and data output.

Architecture Diagram Place Payroll_architecture_diagram.png in the same folder
Academic Governance

Lesson-level tracking against syllabi alongside student engagement. Each file serves as both the source of truth and the operational input.

Branch Operations

Each branch has a consolidated report file that automatically pulls and aggregates data, providing leadership an operational overview.

Financial Automation

Automated payroll derived from actual teaching records across branches. Payslips distributed before pay date to allow timely review and discrepancy reporting.

04

The System

Google Sheets as the data backbone, Apps Script as the automation layer, Google Drive as the delivery mechanism.
Each module is a functional component of the integrated infrastructure.

01 / Data Layer
Data Model

A Galaxy Schema separating session-level and student-level data into distinct Fact tables with four supporting Dimension tables — normalizing pivot-format input sheets into a relational structure for analysis and reporting.

Payroll_data_model.png
02 / Input Layer
Input Sheets UI

The Google Sheets input interface is a pivot-format entry form optimized for minimal friction during data entry, while maintaining structure for downstream aggregation and reporting.

Input_sheets_ui.png
03 / Aggregation Layer
Formula Logic

The Google Sheets formulas automatically aggregate session data from class input files into branch-level reports — handling cross-file references, conditional attendance logic, hour calculation per staff without manual steps.

Formula_logic.png
04 / Automation Layer
Apps Script Automation

The code, combined with spreadsheet formulas, triggers payslip generation, compiles individual salary statements from verified session records, and sends emails to staff before payday — removing manual distribution.

Apps_script_automation.png
05 / Output Layer
Payslip — Staff View

Individual payslip auto-generated and emailed before payday — a session-level breakdown of date, duration, rate, and total gives every staff full transparency and a basis for proactive dispute resolution.

06 / Reporting Layer
Payroll Summary

The consolidated matrix showing total salary costs by staff and branch over any selected time range — enabling leadership to analyse costs per branch and per business line, a capability they did not previously have.

05

Business Value Delivered

Beyond solving immediate pain points, the system laid the data maturity for scalable operations — where clarity grows with the business.
➔ Strategic Foundation: Leadership gained a level of visibility they had never had, while staff benefited from long-awaited transparency.

✔ Operational Intelligence

From Reactive to Real-Time Oversight

Leadership moved from reactive problem-solving to structured monitoring. Branch-level reports surfaced operational issues — session gaps, low attendance, unplanned cancellations — before they escalated into larger failures.

✔ Academic Accountability

Teaching Verification Across Every Session

Per-session topic logs and attendance records created the organization's first audit trail for syllabus adherence. For B2B clients, per-student data enabled structured progress reporting — strengthening contract agreement and customer satisfaction.

✔ Workforce Management

Toward Proactive Resource Planning

As teaching-hour data was consolidated across branches, staff utilization became visible for the first time. This transparency reduced the risk of sudden shortages and enabling better workforce forecasting.

✔ Financial Transparency

Payroll Automated. Disputes Traceable.

Payroll evolved from manual estimation to automated calculation based on verified session records. Early payslip issuance enabled advance review, while disputes dropped sharply as every session was fully traceable.
